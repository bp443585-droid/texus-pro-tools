The Statutory Timeline Matrix: Deposit vs Maturity Blocks

A common computational bottleneck for retail depositors is confusing the active deposit payment timeframe with the absolute runtime of the account ledger. The SSY architecture features a rigid dual-stage block timeline:

Stage 1: The 15-Year Mandatory Deposit Horizon

From the exact day the account is opened, the depositor is legally permitted to make systematic contributions into the ledger for precisely 14 completed fiscal years (up to Year 15). Beyond this 15-year deposit threshold, no fresh out-of-pocket capital injections are accepted by post offices or commercial banks.

Stage 2: The 6-Year Compounding Lock-In Period

From Year 16 through the close of Year 21, the account sits in an exclusive retention phase. Although the parent stops contributing fresh cash, the accumulated principal pool remains fully active inside the sovereign compound interest loop, generating substantial interest updates for an extra 6 years until the account reaches full maturity.

The Mathematics of SSY Annual Compounding Interest Loops

The interest accrual engine inside an SSY ledger operates under an annual compound interest framework, with rates reviewed and set by the government each quarter. Structurally, the interest is calculated monthly on the lowest available balance in the account between the close of the 10th day and the final calendar day of that month.

At the close of each fiscal year (March 31st), the engine compiles the 12 individual monthly interest updates and merges them directly into the primary principal base. The predictive progression across the 21-year asset runtime uses this mathematical structure:

Balance_Year_End = [ Balance_Start + Annual_Deposit ] × (1 + Rate / 100)

During the final 6-year retention phase, because the annual deposit factor matches zero ($Annual\_Deposit = 0$), the formula simplifies to track the compounding returns generated by the accumulated balance alone:

Balance_Retention_Year = Balance_Preceding_Year × (1 + Rate / 100)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - Core Operational Segment

What are the lower and upper deposit limit guidelines for an SSY ledger?

The minimum operational threshold to keep an SSY account active is exactly ₹250 per fiscal year. The absolute statutory maximum limit configuration is capped at ₹1,50,000 per fiscal year. If a parent fails to meet the ₹250 minimum deposit target, the account is placed in default status, which can be reactivated by paying a modest penalty fee of ₹50 per default year.

Can a parent open separate SSY tracking accounts for multiple daughters?

The statutory framework allows a family to open SSY accounts for a maximum of two girl children. An exception is carved out only in the case of twin or triplet births across the first or second delivery sequences, where additional accounts can be activated upon presenting certified medical validation to the post office or bank registry.

Are partial premature liquidity withdrawals permitted before Year 21?

Partial withdrawals up to 50% of the account balance at the close of the preceding fiscal year become accessible once the girl child turns 18 or completes the 10th standard. This partial liquidity window is strictly regulated and must be utilized to fund higher education verification fees or enrollment costs.

Does the account automatically close if the girl child gets married before Year 21?

Yes, if the girl child marries after reaching the legal age of 18, the account can be closed early. You must submit a closure request form along with certified proof of marriage before or immediately after the wedding date. The system will then process a full payout of the accumulated balance up to that date, terminating the ledger.
